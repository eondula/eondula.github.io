Elizabeth Akinyi Ondula ondula@usc.edu

I am a Ph.D. student in Computer Science at the University of Southern California, affiliated with the Autonomous Networks Research Group under the guidance of Professor Bhaskar Krishnamachari. My research is centered around the design and implementation of artificial intelligence systems and tools to tackle decision-making and control tasks in diverse domains. In particular, I utilize reinforcement learning as a framework to model and reason under uncertainties, with a focus on addressing challenges in areas such as safe operations of educational environments and sustainable polyculture systems. Before joining USC, I gained valuable experience as a Research Software Engineer at IBM Research, Africa, as the Head of Product Development at Brave Venture Labs, and as a Co-Lead for Hardware Research at iHub Nairobi. Additionally, I served as a member of the Distributed Ledgers and Artificial Intelligence Taskforce in Kenya. I hold a Bachelor of Engineering degree in Electrical and Electronics Engineering from the Technical University of Kenya.
